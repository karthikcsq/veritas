## 1. PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire-9)

**Full Name:** Patient Health Questionnaire-9
**Purpose:** Screening, diagnosing, monitoring, and measuring severity of depression
**Number of Questions:** 9 (plus 1 functional impairment question not scored)
**Time to Complete:** 2-5 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past 2 weeks
**Validation Status:** Extensively validated; sensitivity 88%, specificity 88% for major depression at cutoff >= 10
**Licensing:** Free to use, no permission required (developed by Drs. Kroenke, Spitzer, Williams; funded by Pfizer)
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Depression treatment trials, mental health screening in any clinical trial, primary care research

### Complete Questions:
Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following problems?

1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things
2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless
3. Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much
4. Feeling tired or having little energy
5. Poor appetite or overeating
6. Feeling bad about yourself - or that you are a failure or have let yourself or your family down
7. Trouble concentrating on things, such as reading the newspaper or watching television
8. Moving or speaking so slowly that other people could have noticed? Or the opposite - being so fidgety or restless that you have been moving around a lot more than usual
9. Thoughts that you would be better off dead, or of hurting yourself in some way

**Functional Question (not scored):** If you checked off any problems, how difficult have these problems made it for you to do your work, take care of things at home, or get along with other people?

### Response Format: 4-point Likert scale
- 0 = Not at all
- 1 = Several days
- 2 = More than half the days
- 3 = Nearly every day

### Scoring:
- Total range: 0-27
- 0-4: None-minimal depression
- 5-9: Mild depression
- 10-14: Moderate depression
- 15-19: Moderately severe depression
- 20-27: Severe depression

### Domains/Subscales:
- Single dimension (depression severity)
- Item 9 serves as suicide risk screening flag
- Functional impairment question provides supplemental clinical information

## 2. GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item)

**Full Name:** Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item Scale
**Purpose:** Screening and measuring severity of generalized anxiety disorder
**Number of Questions:** 7
**Time to Complete:** 2-3 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past 2 weeks
**Validation Status:** Sensitivity 89%, specificity 82% at cutoff >= 10; 92% sensitivity, 76% specificity at cutoff >= 8
**Licensing:** Free to use, no permission required
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Anxiety disorder treatment trials, mental health screening, co-morbidity assessment

### Complete Questions:
Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following problems?

1. Feeling nervous, anxious, or on edge
2. Not being able to stop or control worrying
3. Worrying too much about different things
4. Trouble relaxing
5. Being so restless that it is hard to sit still
6. Becoming easily annoyed or irritable
7. Feeling afraid, as if something awful might happen

### Response Format: 4-point Likert scale
- 0 = Not at all
- 1 = Several days
- 2 = More than half the days
- 3 = Nearly every day

### Scoring:
- Total range: 0-21
- 0-4: Minimal anxiety
- 5-9: Mild anxiety
- 10-14: Moderate anxiety
- 15-21: Severe anxiety

### Domains/Subscales:
- Single dimension (anxiety severity)

## 3. DASS-21 (Depression Anxiety Stress Scales - 21 item)

**Full Name:** Depression Anxiety Stress Scales - Short Form (21 items)
**Purpose:** Measuring three related negative emotional states: depression, anxiety, and stress
**Number of Questions:** 21 (shortened from DASS-42)
**Time to Complete:** 5-10 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past week
**Validation Status:** Well-validated across multiple populations and languages
**Licensing:** Free for research and clinical purposes; available from Psychology Foundation of Australia
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Mental health intervention trials, psychological research, stress-related studies

### Complete Questions:
Please read each statement and select a number 0, 1, 2 or 3 which indicates how much the statement applied to you over the past week.

**Depression Subscale (Items 3, 5, 10, 13, 16, 17, 21):**
1. I couldn't seem to experience any positive feeling at all
2. I found it difficult to work up the initiative to do things
3. I felt that I had nothing to look forward to
4. I felt down-hearted and blue
5. I was unable to become enthusiastic about anything
6. I felt I wasn't worth much as a person
7. I felt that life was meaningless

**Anxiety Subscale (Items 2, 4, 7, 9, 15, 19, 20):**
1. I was aware of dryness of my mouth
2. I experienced breathing difficulty (e.g., excessively rapid breathing, breathlessness in the absence of physical exertion)
3. I experienced trembling (e.g., in the hands)
4. I was worried about situations in which I might panic and make a fool of myself
5. I felt I was close to panic
6. I was aware of the action of my heart in the absence of physical exertion (e.g., sense of heart rate increase, heart missing a beat)
7. I felt scared without any good reason

**Stress Subscale (Items 1, 6, 8, 11, 12, 14, 18):**
1. I found it hard to wind down
2. I tended to over-react to situations
3. I felt that I was using a lot of nervous energy
4. I found myself getting agitated
5. I found it difficult to relax
6. I was intolerant of anything that kept me from getting on with what I was doing
7. I felt that I was rather touchy

### Response Format: 4-point Likert scale
- 0 = Did not apply to me at all
- 1 = Applied to me to some degree, or some of the time
- 2 = Applied to me to a considerable degree, or a good part of the time
- 3 = Applied to me very much, or most of the time

### Scoring:
- Each subscale: sum of 7 items (range 0-21), then multiply by 2 to match DASS-42 norms
- **Depression:** Normal (0-9), Mild (10-13), Moderate (14-20), Severe (21-27), Extremely Severe (28+)
- **Anxiety:** Normal (0-7), Mild (8-9), Moderate (10-14), Severe (15-19), Extremely Severe (20+)
- **Stress:** Normal (0-14), Mild (15-18), Moderate (19-25), Severe (26-33), Extremely Severe (34+)

### Domains/Subscales:
- Depression (7 items): dysphoria, hopelessness, devaluation of life, self-deprecation, lack of interest, anhedonia, inertia
- Anxiety (7 items): autonomic arousal, skeletal muscle effects, situational anxiety, subjective experience of anxious affect
- Stress (7 items): difficulty relaxing, nervous arousal, easily upset/agitated, irritable/over-reactive, impatient

## 4. BDI-II (Beck Depression Inventory - Second Edition)

**Full Name:** Beck Depression Inventory - Second Edition
**Purpose:** Measuring severity of depression
**Number of Questions:** 21
**Time to Complete:** 5-10 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past 2 weeks (including today)
**Validation Status:** Gold standard; internal consistency alpha=0.91, test-retest reliability r=0.93, convergent validity r=0.71 with HAM-D
**Licensing:** COPYRIGHTED - must be purchased from Pearson Assessments; requires licensed professional administration
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Depression treatment trials, psychiatric research, treatment outcome studies
**Age Range:** 13 years and older

### 21 Item Topics (exact wording copyrighted):
Each item presents 4 statements (scored 0-3) from which the respondent selects the one that best describes them:

1. Sadness
2. Pessimism
3. Past Failure
4. Loss of Pleasure
5. Guilty Feelings
6. Punishment Feelings
7. Self-Dislike
8. Self-Criticalness
9. Suicidal Thoughts or Wishes
10. Crying
11. Agitation
12. Loss of Interest
13. Indecisiveness
14. Worthlessness
15. Loss of Energy
16. Changes in Sleeping Pattern
17. Irritability
18. Changes in Appetite
19. Concentration Difficulty
20. Tiredness or Fatigue
21. Loss of Interest in Sex

### Response Format: 4-point scale per item (0-3)
Each item has 4 statements of increasing severity. Example format:
- 0 = I do not feel sad
- 1 = I feel sad much of the time
- 2 = I am sad all the time
- 3 = I am so sad or unhappy that I can't stand it

### Scoring:
- Total range: 0-63
- 0-13: Minimal depression
- 14-19: Mild depression
- 20-28: Moderate depression
- 29-63: Severe depression

## 5. HAM-D / HDRS (Hamilton Depression Rating Scale)

**Full Name:** Hamilton Rating Scale for Depression (also HDRS, HAM-D)
**Purpose:** Clinician-administered measure of depression severity; gold standard for clinical trials
**Number of Questions:** 17 items (original version; 21 and 24 item versions also exist)
**Time to Complete:** 15-20 minutes (clinician-administered interview)
**Recall Period:** Past week
**Validation Status:** The gold standard for depression clinical trials since 1960
**Licensing:** Public domain
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Antidepressant drug trials (primary endpoint), psychiatric research. Score >= 20 typically required for trial entry. Response = >= 50% reduction. Remission = score 0-7.

### Complete 17 Items with Scoring Options:

**1. Depressed Mood** (0-4 scale)
- 0 = Absent
- 1 = Indicated only on questioning (sadness)
- 2 = Spontaneously reported verbally (occasional weeping)
- 3 = Communicated non-verbally (frequent weeping)
- 4 = Virtually only these feelings reported spontaneously

**2. Feelings of Guilt** (0-4 scale)
- 0 = Absent
- 1 = Self-reproach, feels he/she has let people down
- 2 = Ideas of guilt or rumination over past errors
- 3 = Present illness is a punishment; delusions of guilt
- 4 = Hears accusatory or denunciatory voices; hallucinations of guilt

**3. Suicide** (0-4 scale)
- 0 = Absent
- 1 = Feels life is not worth living
- 2 = Wishes he/she were dead
- 3 = Suicidal ideas or gestures
- 4 = Attempts at suicide

**4. Initial Insomnia** (0-2 scale)
- 0 = No difficulty falling asleep
- 1 = Occasional difficulty falling asleep (>30 min)
- 2 = Nightly difficulty falling asleep

**5. Middle Insomnia** (0-2 scale)
- 0 = No difficulty
- 1 = Complains of being restless and disturbed during the night
- 2 = Waking during the night

**6. Delayed Insomnia** (0-2 scale)
- 0 = No difficulty
- 1 = Waking in early hours but goes back to sleep
- 2 = Unable to fall asleep again if gets out of bed

**7. Work and Interests** (0-4 scale)
- 0 = No difficulty
- 1 = Feelings of incapacity, listlessness, indecision, vacillation
- 2 = Loss of interest in hobbies, decreased social activities
- 3 = Decrease in actual productivity
- 4 = Unable to work; stopped working due to present illness only

**8. Retardation** (0-4 scale)
- 0 = Normal speech and thought
- 1 = Slight retardation at interview
- 2 = Obvious retardation at interview
- 3 = Interview difficult
- 4 = Complete stupor

**9. Agitation** (0-4 scale)
- 0 = None
- 1 = Fidgetiness
- 2 = Playing with hands, hair; restlessness
- 3 = Moving about, can't sit still
- 4 = Hand wringing, nail biting, pulling hair

**10. Psychiatric Anxiety** (0-4 scale)
- 0 = No difficulty
- 1 = Subjective tension and irritability
- 2 = Worrying about minor matters
- 3 = Apprehensive attitude apparent in face or speech
- 4 = Fears expressed without questioning

**11. Somatic Anxiety** (0-4 scale)
- 0 = Absent
- 1 = Mild (GI, cardiovascular, respiratory symptoms)
- 2 = Moderate
- 3 = Severe
- 4 = Incapacitating

**12. Gastrointestinal Somatic Symptoms** (0-2 scale)
- 0 = None
- 1 = Loss of appetite but eating without encouragement; heavy feelings in abdomen
- 2 = Difficulty eating without urging; requests laxatives

**13. General Somatic Symptoms** (0-2 scale)
- 0 = None
- 1 = Heaviness in limbs, back, head; loss of energy, fatigability
- 2 = Any clear-cut symptom

**14. Genital Symptoms** (0-2 scale)
- 0 = Not present
- 1 = Mild (loss of libido)
- 2 = Severe (menstrual disturbances)

**15. Hypochondriasis** (0-4 scale)
- 0 = Not present
- 1 = Self-absorption (bodily)
- 2 = Preoccupation with health
- 3 = Frequent complaints, requests for help
- 4 = Hypochondriacal delusions

**16. Weight Loss** (0-2 scale)
- 0 = No weight loss
- 1 = Probable weight loss
- 2 = Definite weight loss

**17. Insight** (0-2 scale)
- 0 = Acknowledges being depressed and ill
- 1 = Acknowledges illness but attributes cause to bad food, climate, overwork, etc.
- 2 = Denies being ill at all

### Scoring:
- Total range: 0-52
- 0-7: Normal / Clinical remission
- 8-13: Mild depression
- 14-18: Moderate depression
- 19-22: Severe depression
- >= 23: Very severe depression

### Key Notes:
- CLINICIAN-ADMINISTERED (not self-report like PHQ-9 or BDI-II)
- 8 items scored 0-4, 9 items scored 0-2
- Treatment response in trials = >= 50% reduction from baseline
- Score >= 20 typically required for clinical trial entry

## 6. SF-36 (Short Form 36 Health Survey)

**Full Name:** 36-Item Short Form Health Survey (also RAND-36)
**Purpose:** Measuring general health-related quality of life across 8 domains
**Number of Questions:** 36
**Time to Complete:** 5-10 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past 4 weeks (most items)
**Validation Status:** One of most widely used HRQoL instruments; used in thousands of studies
**Licensing:** RAND-36 version is free/public domain. SF-36v2 from QualityMetric requires license fee.
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Any disease area; baseline/follow-up HRQoL assessment; health economic evaluations

### 8 Domains with Item Counts:
1. **Physical Functioning (PF)** - 10 items: Limitations in physical activities (vigorous activities, moderate activities, lifting/carrying groceries, climbing several flights of stairs, climbing one flight of stairs, bending/kneeling/stooping, walking more than a mile, walking several hundred yards, walking one hundred yards, bathing/dressing yourself)
2. **Role-Physical (RP)** - 4 items: Problems with work or other daily activities due to physical health
3. **Bodily Pain (BP)** - 2 items: Pain severity and interference
4. **General Health (GH)** - 5 items: General health perceptions
5. **Vitality (VT)** - 4 items: Energy and fatigue
6. **Social Functioning (SF)** - 2 items: Interference with normal social activities
7. **Role-Emotional (RE)** - 3 items: Problems with work or activities due to emotional problems
8. **Mental Health (MH)** - 5 items: Psychological distress and well-being
9. **Health Transition** - 1 item (not scored in 8 domains): Perceived change in health

### Sample Questions:
- Q1 (General Health): "In general, would you say your health is: Excellent / Very Good / Good / Fair / Poor"
- Q3a-j (Physical Functioning): "Does your health now limit you in [activity]? Yes, Limited a Lot / Yes, Limited a Little / No, Not Limited at All"
- Q7 (Bodily Pain): "How much bodily pain have you had during the past 4 weeks?"
- Q9a-i (Vitality/Mental Health): "How much of the time during the past 4 weeks..."

### Response Formats (mixed):
- 3-point Likert (Physical Functioning items): Yes limited a lot / Yes limited a little / No not limited at all
- 5-point Likert (General Health, Vitality, Mental Health items)
- 6-point frequency scale: All of the time / Most of the time / A good bit of the time / Some of the time / A little of the time / None of the time
- Yes/No (Role limitation items in original version)

### Scoring:
- All items recoded to 0-100 scale (higher = better health)
- Domain scores = average of items within each domain (0-100)
- Two summary scores: Physical Component Summary (PCS) and Mental Component Summary (MCS)
- Norm-based scoring: mean=50, SD=10 for general US population

## 7. EQ-5D-5L

**Full Name:** EuroQol 5 Dimensions 5 Levels
**Purpose:** Standardized measure of health-related quality of life for clinical and economic appraisal
**Number of Questions:** 5 dimension questions + 1 Visual Analogue Scale (VAS)
**Time to Complete:** 2-3 minutes
**Recall Period:** Today ("your health TODAY")
**Validation Status:** Extensively validated; used in >20,000 studies; available in 200+ languages
**Licensing:** REQUIRES LICENSE from EuroQol Group; fees vary by study type
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Health economic evaluations, cost-effectiveness analyses, any disease area, utility measurement for QALYs

### 5 Dimensions:

**1. MOBILITY**
- I have no problems in walking about
- I have slight problems in walking about
- I have moderate problems in walking about
- I have severe problems in walking about
- I am unable to walk about

**2. SELF-CARE**
- I have no problems washing or dressing myself
- I have slight problems washing or dressing myself
- I have moderate problems washing or dressing myself
- I have severe problems washing or dressing myself
- I am unable to wash or dress myself

**3. USUAL ACTIVITIES** (e.g. work, study, housework, family or leisure activities)
- I have no problems doing my usual activities
- I have slight problems doing my usual activities
- I have moderate problems doing my usual activities
- I have severe problems doing my usual activities
- I am unable to do my usual activities

**4. PAIN / DISCOMFORT**
- I have no pain or discomfort
- I have slight pain or discomfort
- I have moderate pain or discomfort
- I have severe pain or discomfort
- I have extreme pain or discomfort

**5. ANXIETY / DEPRESSION**
- I am not anxious or depressed
- I am slightly anxious or depressed
- I am moderately anxious or depressed
- I am severely anxious or depressed
- I am extremely anxious or depressed

### EQ VAS:
- Vertical 20cm visual analogue scale
- Anchored at: 0 = "The worst health you can imagine" to 100 = "The best health you can imagine"
- Patient marks an X on the scale

### Response Format: 5-level ordinal per dimension
### Scoring:
- Health state profile: 5-digit code (e.g., 11111 = perfect health, 55555 = worst state)
- 3,125 possible health states (5^5)
- Value sets convert profiles to utility index (country-specific; typically -0.5 to 1.0)
- EQ VAS score: 0-100

## 8. WHOQOL-BREF

**Full Name:** World Health Organization Quality of Life - Brief Version
**Purpose:** Cross-cultural assessment of quality of life
**Number of Questions:** 26
**Time to Complete:** 5-10 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past 2 weeks
**Validation Status:** Validated in 23+ countries; field-tested internationally by WHO
**Licensing:** Free but requires user agreement from WHO; contact whoqol@who.int
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** International/cross-cultural clinical trials, chronic disease management, mental health research

### 4 Domains + 2 Global Items:

**Overall QoL and General Health (2 items):**
- Q1: How would you rate your quality of life? (Very poor / Poor / Neither poor nor good / Good / Very good)
- Q2: How satisfied are you with your health? (Very dissatisfied / Dissatisfied / Neither / Satisfied / Very satisfied)

**Domain 1 - Physical Health (7 items: Q3, Q4, Q10, Q15, Q16, Q17, Q18):**
Facets: pain & discomfort, dependence on medication, energy & fatigue, mobility, sleep & rest, activities of daily living, work capacity
- Q3: To what extent do you feel that (physical) pain prevents you from doing what you need to do?
- Q4: How much do you need any medical treatment to function in your daily life?
- Q10: Do you have enough energy for everyday life?
- Q15: How well are you able to get around?
- Q16: How satisfied are you with your sleep?
- Q17: How satisfied are you with your ability to perform your daily living activities?
- Q18: How satisfied are you with your capacity for work?

**Domain 2 - Psychological (6 items: Q5, Q6, Q7, Q11, Q19, Q26):**
Facets: positive feelings, spirituality/personal beliefs, thinking/learning/memory/concentration, body image/appearance, self-esteem, negative feelings
- Q5: How much do you enjoy life?
- Q6: To what extent do you feel your life to be meaningful?
- Q7: How well are you able to concentrate?
- Q11: Are you able to accept your bodily appearance?
- Q19: How satisfied are you with yourself?
- Q26: How often do you have negative feelings such as blue mood, despair, anxiety, depression?

**Domain 3 - Social Relationships (3 items: Q20, Q21, Q22):**
Facets: personal relationships, social support, sexual activity
- Q20: How satisfied are you with your personal relationships?
- Q21: How satisfied are you with your sex life?
- Q22: How satisfied are you with the support you get from your friends?

**Domain 4 - Environment (8 items: Q8, Q9, Q12, Q13, Q14, Q23, Q24, Q25):**
Facets: physical safety/security, physical environment, financial resources, information availability, leisure, home environment, health care access, transport
- Q8: How safe do you feel in your daily life?
- Q9: How healthy is your physical environment?
- Q12: Have you enough money to meet your needs?
- Q13: How available to you is the information that you need in your day-to-day life?
- Q14: To what extent do you have the opportunity for leisure activities?
- Q23: How satisfied are you with the conditions of your living place?
- Q24: How satisfied are you with your access to health services?
- Q25: How satisfied are you with your transport?

### Response Format: 5-point Likert scales (various anchors depending on question type)
- Intensity: Not at all / A little / A moderate amount / Very much / An extreme amount
- Capacity: Not at all / A little / Moderately / Mostly / Completely
- Frequency: Never / Seldom / Quite often / Very often / Always
- Satisfaction: Very dissatisfied / Dissatisfied / Neither / Satisfied / Very satisfied

### Scoring:
- Items scored 1-5
- Items Q3, Q4, Q26 are reverse-scored (6-x)
- Domain raw scores = mean of items in domain
- Transformed scores: multiply by 4 to get 4-20 range, or convert to 0-100 scale
- Higher scores = better QoL
- Cannot evaluate if >20% questions missing

## 9. VAS (Visual Analog Scale) for Pain

**Full Name:** Visual Analog Scale
**Purpose:** Measuring pain intensity
**Number of Questions:** 1
**Time to Complete:** <1 minute
**Validation Status:** Extensively validated; widely used for decades
**Licensing:** Public domain; no license required
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Pain treatment trials, post-surgical assessment, any trial measuring pain

### Description:
A 100mm horizontal line with anchors at each end:
- Left anchor: "No pain" (0)
- Right anchor: "Worst pain imaginable" (100)
- Patient marks a point on the line
- Distance from left end measured in millimeters

### Response Format: Continuous scale (0-100mm)

### Scoring:
- Measured in mm from left anchor
- 0-4mm: No pain
- 5-44mm: Mild pain
- 45-74mm: Moderate pain
- 75-100mm: Severe pain

### Key Note: FDA does NOT recommend VAS for clinical trials; prefers NRS

---

## 10. NRS (Numeric Rating Scale) for Pain

**Full Name:** Numeric Rating Scale
**Purpose:** Measuring pain intensity (FDA-preferred for clinical trials)
**Number of Questions:** 1
**Time to Complete:** <1 minute
**Validation Status:** FDA-recommended; superior to VAS in compliance and psychometric retention
**Licensing:** Public domain
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Pain drug trials (FDA-preferred primary endpoint), post-operative pain, chronic pain studies

### Question:
"On a scale of 0 to 10, where 0 is no pain and 10 is the worst pain you can imagine, how would you rate your pain right now?"

Variations: "worst pain in past 24 hours," "average pain in past 24 hours," "least pain in past 24 hours"

### Response Format: 11-point numeric scale (0-10)

### Scoring:
- 0: No pain
- 1-3: Mild pain
- 4-6: Moderate pain
- 7-10: Severe pain

## 11. BPI-SF (Brief Pain Inventory - Short Form)

**Full Name:** Brief Pain Inventory - Short Form
**Purpose:** Assessing pain severity and pain interference with daily functions
**Number of Questions:** 9 main items (plus body diagram and treatment questions)
**Time to Complete:** 5 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past 24 hours
**Validation Status:** Extensively validated in cancer and non-cancer pain; 2000+ citations
**Licensing:** Free for non-commercial use; available from MD Anderson Cancer Center
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Cancer pain trials, chronic pain studies, analgesic efficacy trials

### Questions:

**Pain Drawing:** Body diagram for indicating pain locations

**Pain Severity (4 items, 0-10 NRS each):**
1. Pain at its worst in the last 24 hours (0=No pain, 10=Pain as bad as you can imagine)
2. Pain at its least in the last 24 hours
3. Pain on the average
4. Pain right now

**Treatment Questions (2 items):**
5. Treatments or medications received
6. Percentage of pain relief from treatments (0-100%)

**Pain Interference (7 sub-items, 0-10 NRS each):**
7. During the past 24 hours, how much has pain interfered with your:
   a. General activity
   b. Mood
   c. Walking ability
   d. Normal work (includes both work outside the home and housework)
   e. Relations with other people
   f. Sleep
   g. Enjoyment of life

### Response Format: 0-10 Numeric Rating Scale

### Scoring:
- Pain Severity Score: mean of items 1-4 (range 0-10)
- Pain Interference Score: mean of 7 interference sub-items (range 0-10)
- "Pain at its worst" single item is FDA-recommended endpoint

### Domains:
- Pain Severity (sensory dimension)
- Pain Interference (reactive dimension)

## 12. McGill Pain Questionnaire (MPQ)

**Full Name:** McGill Pain Questionnaire (also SF-MPQ for Short Form)
**Purpose:** Comprehensive multidimensional assessment of pain quality and intensity
**Number of Questions:** 78 descriptors in 20 subgroups (long form); 15 descriptors (short form)
**Time to Complete:** 15-20 minutes (long form); 2-5 minutes (short form)
**Validation Status:** Validated across numerous pain conditions
**Licensing:** Copyrighted; permission required from Ronald Melzack estate/McGill University
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Chronic pain research, neuropathic pain studies, pain characterization

### Structure (Long Form):
78 pain descriptors organized into 20 subgroups across 4 major categories:

**Sensory (Subgroups 1-10):**
Temporal: flickering, quivering, pulsing, throbbing, beating, pounding
Spatial: jumping, flashing, shooting
Punctate pressure: pricking, boring, drilling, stabbing, lancinating
Incisive pressure: sharp, cutting, lacerating
Constrictive: pinching, pressing, gnawing, cramping, crushing
Traction: tugging, pulling, wrenching
Thermal: hot, burning, scalding, searing
Brightness: tingling, itchy, smarting, stinging
Dullness: dull, sore, hurting, aching, heavy
Sensory-miscellaneous: tender, taut, rasping, splitting

**Affective (Subgroups 11-15):**
Tension: tiring, exhausting
Autonomic: sickening, suffocating
Fear: fearful, frightful, terrifying
Punishment: punishing, gruelling, cruel, vicious, killing
Affective-evaluative: wretched, blinding

**Evaluative (Subgroup 16):**
Annoying, troublesome, miserable, intense, unbearable

**Miscellaneous (Subgroups 17-20):**
Spreading, radiating, penetrating, piercing
Tight, numb, drawing, squeezing, tearing
Cool, cold, freezing
Nagging, nauseating, agonizing, dreadful, torturing

### Short Form (SF-MPQ) - 15 descriptors rated 0-3:
Sensory: throbbing, shooting, stabbing, sharp, cramping, gnawing, hot-burning, aching, heavy, tender, splitting
Affective: tiring-exhausting, sickening, fearful, punishing-cruel

### Scoring Methods:
1. **Pain Rating Index (PRI):** Sum of rank values for chosen words
2. **Number of Words Chosen (NWC):** Count of selected descriptors
3. **Present Pain Intensity (PPI):** 0-5 intensity scale (0=no pain to 5=excruciating)
- Maximum total score: 78 (long form)
- SF-MPQ range: 0-45

## 13. EORTC QLQ-C30 (version 3)

**Full Name:** European Organisation for Research and Treatment of Cancer Quality of Life Questionnaire - Core 30
**Purpose:** Multidimensional assessment of quality of life in cancer patients
**Number of Questions:** 30
**Time to Complete:** 11-12 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past week (most items)
**Validation Status:** The standard QoL instrument in European cancer trials; used since 1993
**Licensing:** Free for academic research; requires registration with EORTC Quality of Life Group
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** All oncology clinical trials; often required by regulatory agencies for cancer drug approval

### Structure - 15 Subscales:

**5 Functional Scales:**
1. Physical functioning (5 items: Q1-Q5)
2. Role functioning (2 items: Q6-Q7)
3. Emotional functioning (4 items: Q21-Q24)

### Sample Questions (from specimen form):

**Purpose:** Measuring health-related quality of life in cancer patients
**Number of Questions:** 27
**Time to Complete:** 5-10 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past 7 days
**Validation Status:** Extensively validated; Cronbach alpha 0.89; test-retest reliability 0.92
**Licensing:** Free for academic/non-commercial use; requires registration at FACIT.org
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Oncology clinical trials, cancer survivorship research

### 4 Domains:

**Physical Well-Being (PWB) - 7 items (score range 0-28):**
- GP1: I have a lack of energy
- GP2: I have nausea

- GS4: My family has accepted my illness
- GS5: I am satisfied with family communication about my illness
- GS6: I feel close to my partner (or the person who is my main support)

- GE4: I feel nervous
- GE5: I worry about dying

## 16. PSQI (Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index)

**Full Name:** Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index
**Purpose:** Assessing sleep quality and disturbances over a 1-month period
**Number of Questions:** 19 self-rated items (+ 5 bed partner items, not scored)
**Time to Complete:** 5-10 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past month
**Validation Status:** Most widely used sleep assessment; diagnostic sensitivity 89.6%, specificity 86.5% at cutoff >5
**Licensing:** Free for non-commercial research/educational use; owned by University of Pittsburgh
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Sleep disorder trials, insomnia treatment, medication side-effect monitoring, substance use disorder trials

### 7 Component Scores:

**Component 1 - Subjective Sleep Quality:**
- "During the past month, how would you rate your sleep quality overall?"
- Very good / Fairly good / Fairly bad / Very bad

**Component 2 - Sleep Latency:**
- "During the past month, how long (in minutes) has it usually taken you to fall asleep each night?"
- "During the past month, how often have you had trouble sleeping because you cannot get to sleep within 30 minutes?"

**Component 3 - Sleep Duration:**
- "During the past month, how many hours of actual sleep did you get at night? (This may be different from the number of hours you spend in bed)"

**Component 4 - Habitual Sleep Efficiency:**
- Calculated from: "During the past month, when have you usually gone to bed at night?"
- And: "During the past month, when have you usually gotten up in the morning?"
- Plus sleep duration
- Sleep efficiency = (hours slept / hours in bed) x 100%

**Component 5 - Sleep Disturbances (9 sub-items):**
During the past month, how often have you had trouble sleeping because you...
a. Cannot get to sleep within 30 minutes
b. Wake up in the middle of the night or early morning
c. Have to get up to use the bathroom
d. Cannot breathe comfortably
e. Cough or snore loudly
f. Feel too cold
g. Feel too hot
h. Had bad dreams
i. Have pain
j. Other reason(s)

**Component 6 - Use of Sleeping Medication:**
- "During the past month, how often have you taken medicine (prescribed or 'over the counter') to help you sleep?"

**Component 7 - Daytime Dysfunction:**
- "During the past month, how often have you had trouble staying awake while driving, eating meals, or engaging in social activity?"
- "During the past month, how much of a problem has it been for you to keep up enough enthusiasm to get things done?"

### Response Formats: Mixed
- Free text (bedtime, wake time, sleep duration, sleep latency in minutes)
- 4-point frequency scale: Not during the past month (0) / Less than once a week (1) / Once or twice a week (2) / Three or more times a week (3)

### Scoring:
- Each of 7 components scored 0-3
- Global PSQI score = sum of 7 components (range 0-21)
- Score > 5: Poor sleep quality (significant sleep difficulties)
- Score <= 5: Good sleep quality
- Lower scores = better sleep

## 17. ISI (Insomnia Severity Index)

**Full Name:** Insomnia Severity Index
**Purpose:** Screening for insomnia and measuring treatment response
**Number of Questions:** 7
**Time to Complete:** 2-3 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past 2 weeks
**Validation Status:** Well-validated; widely used in CBT-I trials and pharmacotherapy trials
**Licensing:** Free for clinical and research use; developed by Charles M. Morin, PhD
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Insomnia treatment trials (CBT-I, pharmacotherapy), sleep medication trials

### Complete 7 Items:

1. Difficulty falling asleep
   - 0=None, 1=Mild, 2=Moderate, 3=Severe, 4=Very Severe

2. Difficulty staying asleep
   - 0=None, 1=Mild, 2=Moderate, 3=Severe, 4=Very Severe

3. Problems waking up too early
   - 0=None, 1=Mild, 2=Moderate, 3=Severe, 4=Very Severe

4. How SATISFIED/DISSATISFIED are you with your CURRENT sleep pattern?
   - 0=Very Satisfied, 1=Satisfied, 2=Moderately Satisfied, 3=Dissatisfied, 4=Very Dissatisfied

5. How NOTICEABLE to others do you think your sleep problem is in terms of impairing the quality of your life?
   - 0=Not at all Noticeable, 1=A Little, 2=Somewhat, 3=Much, 4=Very Much Noticeable

6. How WORRIED/DISTRESSED are you about your current sleep problem?
   - 0=Not at all Worried, 1=A Little, 2=Somewhat, 3=Much, 4=Very Much

7. To what extent do you consider your sleep problem to INTERFERE with your daily functioning (e.g., daytime fatigue, mood, ability to function at work/daily chores, concentration, memory, mood, etc.) CURRENTLY?
   - 0=Not at all Interfering, 1=A Little, 2=Somewhat, 3=Much, 4=Very Much Interfering

### Response Format: 5-point Likert scale (0-4)
### Scoring:
- Total range: 0-28
- 0-7: No clinically significant insomnia
- 8-14: Subthreshold insomnia
- 15-21: Clinical insomnia (moderate severity)
- 22-28: Clinical insomnia (severe)

## 18. BFI (Brief Fatigue Inventory)

**Full Name:** Brief Fatigue Inventory
**Purpose:** Rapid assessment of fatigue severity and impact
**Number of Questions:** 9
**Time to Complete:** <5 minutes
**Recall Period:** Past 24 hours (severity); current (fatigue now)
**Validation Status:** High internal reliability; validated in cancer and non-cancer populations; 2000+ citations
**Licensing:** Free for non-commercial use from MD Anderson Cancer Center
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Cancer clinical trials, fatigue assessment in oncology, radiotherapy studies

### Complete 9 Items:

**Severity Items (3 questions):**
1. Please rate your fatigue (weariness, tiredness) by circling the one number that best describes your fatigue RIGHT NOW (0-10)
2. Please rate your fatigue (weariness, tiredness) by circling the one number that best describes your USUAL level of fatigue during past 24 hours (0-10)
3. Please rate your fatigue (weariness, tiredness) by circling the one number that best describes your WORST level of fatigue during past 24 hours (0-10)

**Interference Items (6 questions):**
Circle the one number that describes how, during the past 24 hours, fatigue has interfered with your:
4. General activity (0-10)
5. Mood (0-10)
6. Walking ability (0-10)
7. Normal work (includes both work outside the home and housework) (0-10)
8. Relations with other people (0-10)
9. Enjoyment of life (0-10)

### Response Format: 0-10 Numeric Rating Scale
- 0 = No fatigue / Does not interfere
- 10 = As bad as you can imagine / Completely interferes

### Scoring:
- Global fatigue score = mean of all 9 items (range 0-10)
- Severity categories: Mild (1-3), Moderate (4-6), Severe (7-10)
- Can also calculate separate severity and interference means

## 19. MMAS-8 (Morisky Medication Adherence Scale - 8 item)

**Full Name:** Morisky Medication Adherence Scale - 8 Item
**Purpose:** Assessing medication-taking behavior and adherence
**Number of Questions:** 8
**Time to Complete:** 2-3 minutes
**Validation Status:** Widely validated across chronic conditions (hypertension, diabetes, HIV, asthma)
**Licensing:** COPYRIGHTED - License required from MMAR, LLC (Donald Morisky); fees apply
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Any trial requiring adherence monitoring, hypertension trials, chronic disease management

### 8 Questions:

1. Do you sometimes forget to take your [medication]? (Yes/No)
2. People sometimes miss taking their medications for reasons other than forgetting. Thinking over the past two weeks, were there any days when you did not take your [medication]? (Yes/No)
3. Have you ever cut back or stopped taking your [medication] without telling your doctor, because you felt worse when you took it? (Yes/No)
4. When you travel or leave home, do you sometimes forget to bring along your [medication]? (Yes/No)
5. Did you take your [medication] yesterday? (Yes/No) - NOTE: reverse-scored
6. When you feel like your [condition] is under control, do you sometimes stop taking your [medication]? (Yes/No)
7. Taking medication every day is a real inconvenience for some people. Do you ever feel hassled about sticking to your treatment plan? (Yes/No)
8. How often do you have difficulty remembering to take all your medications?
   - Never/Rarely = 0
   - Once in a while = 1
   - Sometimes = 2
   - Usually = 3
   - All the time = 4

### Response Format: Yes/No for items 1-7; 5-point Likert for item 8
### Scoring:
- Items 1-7: "No" = 1, "Yes" = 0 (except item 5: "Yes" = 1, "No" = 0)
- Item 8: Score divided by 4 to standardize (0-1 range)
- Total range: 0-8
- High adherence: 8
- Medium adherence: 6-7
- Low adherence: <6

## 21. PRO-CTCAE (Patient-Reported Outcomes version of Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events)

**Full Name:** Patient-Reported Outcomes version of the Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events
**Purpose:** Patient self-reporting of symptomatic adverse events in cancer clinical trials
**Number of Items:** 124 items representing 78 symptomatic toxicities
**Time to Complete:** Variable (typically 3-8 minutes depending on items selected)
**Recall Period:** Past 7 days
**Validation Status:** Developed by NCI; validated in 60+ languages; psychometric properties well-established
**Licensing:** Free; available from NCI (National Cancer Institute)
**Typical Clinical Trial Context:** Cancer clinical trials (companion to clinician-graded CTCAE)

### Item Structure:
Each symptomatic toxicity has 1-3 items measuring:

**Frequency:** "In the last 7 days, how OFTEN did you have [symptom]?"
- Never / Rarely / Occasionally / Frequently / Almost constantly (0-4)

**Severity:** "In the last 7 days, what was the SEVERITY of your [symptom] at its worst?"
- None / Mild / Moderate / Severe / Very severe (0-4)

**Interference:** "In the last 7 days, how much did [symptom] INTERFERE with your usual or daily activities?"
- Not at all / A little bit / Somewhat / Quite a bit / Very much (0-4)

**Amount:** (for some items) "In the last 7 days, how much [symptom] did you have?"
**Presence/Absence:** (for some items) Yes/No (0/1)

### 78 Symptomatic Toxicities Include (examples):
- Nausea, Vomiting, Diarrhea, Constipation
- Fatigue, Pain, Headache
- Mouth/throat sores, Dry mouth, Taste changes
- Numbness/tingling in hands/feet (neuropathy)
- Hair loss (alopecia)
- Rash, Hand-foot syndrome, Skin dryness
- Decreased appetite, Weight changes
- Shortness of breath, Cough
- Insomnia, Anxiety, Sad/unhappy feelings
- Concentration/memory problems
- Arm/leg swelling (edema)
- Hot flashes, Dizziness
- Blurred vision, Tearing/watery eyes
- Urinary problems, Sexual function changes

### Conditional Branching Logic:
- If frequency > "Never" --> ask severity
- If severity > "None" --> ask interference
- This minimizes respondent burden

### Scoring:
- Individual items scored 0-4 (or 0/1 for presence/absence)
- No standardized composite scoring rules yet
- Scores presented descriptively per attribute alongside CTCAE grades

# SECTION 9: SURVEY CATEGORIES IN CLINICAL TRIALS

---

## A. Screening/Eligibility Surveys

**Purpose:** Determine if potential participants meet inclusion/exclusion criteria

**Common instruments used at screening:**
- PHQ-9 (depression screening - e.g., score >= 10 required for depression trial entry)
- HAM-D (score >= 20 often required for antidepressant trial entry)
- NRS Pain (e.g., average pain >= 4 for chronic pain trial entry)
- MMSE/MoCA (cognitive screening for dementia trials)
- Medical history questionnaires (custom)
- Substance use screening (AUDIT, DAST)

**Typical screening questions include:**
- Demographics (age, sex, ethnicity)
- Medical history review
- Current medications
- Prior treatment history
- Pregnancy status
- Allergies
- Physical exam findings (height, weight, BMI, BP, HR)

---

## B. Baseline Assessment Surveys

**Purpose:** Establish pre-treatment measurements for comparison

**Standard baseline battery may include:**
- Primary outcome measure (disease-specific PRO)
- Quality of life (EQ-5D-5L, SF-36)
- Depression/anxiety screening (PHQ-9, GAD-7)
- Pain assessment (NRS, BPI)
- Sleep quality (PSQI, ISI)
- Fatigue (FACIT-Fatigue, BFI)
- Functional status (ECOG Performance Status)
- Treatment expectations questionnaire

---

## C. Follow-up/Monitoring Surveys

**Purpose:** Track changes over treatment course

**Typically include:**
- Repeat of baseline PRO measures at set intervals
- Adverse event monitoring (PRO-CTCAE)
- Medication adherence (MMAS-8, MARS-5)
- Treatment satisfaction
- Global impression of change (PGIC)

**Patient Global Impression of Change (PGIC):**
Single item: "Since beginning treatment, how would you describe the change in your [condition]?"
- 1 = Very much improved
- 2 = Much improved
- 3 = Minimally improved
- 4 = No change
- 5 = Minimally worse
- 6 = Much worse
- 7 = Very much worse

---

## D. End-of-Study Surveys

**Purpose:** Final assessment and treatment satisfaction

**Typically include:**
- Final repeat of all baseline PRO measures
- Patient satisfaction questionnaire (PSQ-18)
- Treatment preference/willingness to continue
- Global benefit-risk assessment
- Open-ended feedback

---

## E. Informed Consent Comprehension Checks

**Purpose:** Verify participant understanding of trial procedures and risks

**Format:** Typically 10-20 item true/false or multiple-choice quiz
**Passing threshold:** Usually 80% correct (e.g., 16/20 in HIV/TB trials)
**May require re-assessment:** Some trials allow 2-3 attempts

**Common question topics:**
- Study purpose and procedures
- Risks and potential side effects
- Right to withdraw at any time
- Alternatives to participation
- Confidentiality protections
- Compensation and costs
- Contact information for questions
- Understanding of randomization and placebo
- Duration of participation

---

# SECTION 11: COMMON QUESTION TYPES IN CLINICAL TRIAL SURVEYS

---

## 1. Likert Scales

**3-point:** (e.g., EORTC physical functioning)
- Yes, limited a lot / Yes, limited a little / No, not limited at all

**4-point:** (e.g., EORTC QLQ-C30 symptoms)
- Not at all / A little / Quite a bit / Very much

**5-point:** (e.g., EQ-5D-5L, FACT-G, WHOQOL-BREF, PRO-CTCAE)
- Various anchors (None-Extreme, Not at all-Very much, Never-Always, etc.)

**7-point:** (e.g., EORTC global health, PGIC)
- Very poor to Excellent; Very much improved to Very much worse

## 2. Visual Analog Scales (0-100)
- Continuous line, patient marks a point
- Used in: EQ VAS, VAS for pain, VAS for fatigue
- Measured in millimeters

## 3. Numeric Rating Scales (0-10)
- Discrete integer scale
- Used in: NRS pain, BPI, BFI
- FDA-preferred for pain trials

## 4. Yes/No/Don't Know
- Used in: MMAS-8 (items 1-7), SF-36 role limitations (original)
- Binary response

## 5. Multiple Choice
- Used in: Screening questionnaires, demographics, medical history
- Select one or multiple options

## 6. Free Text
- Used in: Open-ended adverse event descriptions, PSQI (bedtime/wake time)
- Qualitative data collection

## 7. Date/Time Responses
- Used in: PSQI (bedtime, wake time), medication diaries
- Structured format

## 8. Body Diagrams
- Used in: BPI (pain location), McGill Pain Questionnaire
- Patient marks/shades areas of pain on body outline
